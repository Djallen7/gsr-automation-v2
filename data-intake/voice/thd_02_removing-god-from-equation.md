Removing God From The Equation

(Sitting at desk with old books)

You know… throughout history, people have wrestled with one big question:

Do we answer to someone… or are we on our own?

Back in the 1800s, the well-known preacher Charles Spurgeon made a powerful observation.

Talking about those who believe that we are just accidents of nature, he said:

"They inform us that by some means this world and all that is therein were evolved… but they care nothing for evolution itself—only so far as it may serve their purpose of escaping from the thought of God."

That's a strong statement.

But when you really think about it… it speaks to something deeper in human nature.

Because if there is no Creator… then there's no ultimate authority.

No higher accountability.

And suddenly… we become the ones who decide what's right… and what's wrong.

Now, on the surface, that might sound freeing.

But history—and even modern culture—shows us how horrible that can actually be.

When moral truth becomes flexible… so does the value of human life.

We begin to redefine things.

Who matters… and who doesn't.

The unborn… the elderly… the vulnerable.

And instead of protecting life… society starts deciding which lives are "worth" living.

But here's the contrast.

The framework given in Scripture is remarkably simple… and incredibly profound.

Love God.

Love your neighbor.

In fact, the principles found in the Ten Commandments have shaped entire civilizations—forming the basis for laws, justice systems, and human rights across much of the world.

And yet… none of us live that standard perfectly.

Not one.

That's why the message of the Gospel is so powerful.

Because it's about redemption.

God didn't leave us to figure it out alone.

He stepped into His creation… and made a way for us.

So the question isn't just…

"Can we remove God from the equation?"

The real question is…What happens when we do?

Because when we remove the Designer… we don't just lose authority—we lose meaning.
