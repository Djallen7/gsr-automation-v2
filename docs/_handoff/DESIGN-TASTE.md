# DESIGN-TASTE.md — Daniel's visual taste, in his own reactions

**This file is the ONLY visual design guidance in this repo** (fresh start declared by
Daniel, 2026-06-12). Everything older — mockups, the bake-off, research briefs, theme
syntheses, donor maps, token sheets, screen blueprints — is archived in
`docs/_archive/2026-06-12-ui-fresh-start/` and is NOT guidance. Do not design from
that folder. Web research and design skills may inform proposals, but a rule only
enters this file when Daniel reacts.

Append-only, dated. Every reaction Daniel gives — including every "no, I don't like
that" — becomes a rule here so the same idea is never re-tested on him twice.

## Taste rules (from Daniel)

**2026-06-12 (fresh-start message, reviewing on his phone):**

1. **No walls of numbers.** "I cannot look at all of those numbers and not get
   overwhelmed." Front screens carry no stat grids, no KPI strips, no count tiles.
   Visual elements tell the story on the dashboard's behalf; numbers are detail,
   shown one tap deeper, only when asked for.
2. **Cleaner beats richer.** Of the four 2026-06-12 mocks, Soft Structural felt best
   ("it feels cleaner"). Seen on phone; desktop re-check pending. The earlier one-tap
   Liquid Glass approval is RESCINDED. No mock is approved.
3. **Every element needs a reason.** "Every element has to be there for a reason, and
   it all has to be cohesive." If an element's reason cannot be stated in one line
   tied to a question Daniel actually asks of the dashboard, it does not ship.
4. **Not generic either.** A stripped, template-looking admin panel is rejected in
   advance ("I also don't want, like, a generic dashboard... and ugly"). Character
   should come from the show's real content (episode titles, guests, air dates), not
   decorative widgets.
5. **Phone first.** Daniel reviews on his phone. Judge every design there first;
   desktop is the second pass.

**2026-06-12 (second message, the centralization problem):**

6. **Home page last.** Daniel has not decided what he wants to see, and "what makes
   sense on a home page" is the wrong question. Build the responsibility tracks first;
   the home screen gets composed later from what he actually uses. Do not pitch
   home-screen layouts until the producer month map exists.
7. **The today-view seed is his own words.** The only confirmed "today" content is his
   daily todo list synced from his notes. Self-authored content first; inferred or
   prioritized content earns its place track by track.
8. **The five-episode status grid on the home page: doubted.** (The Episodes screen
   itself still stands; this is specifically about home-page content.)
9. **Trust by evidence, never by claim.** No black-box priority lists. Anything the
   dashboard asserts (a deadline, a waiting-on, a flag) shows its evidence inline and is
   correctable in one tap, and corrections are remembered. Booking lives in an explicit
   tracker with states; email is attached evidence, never the source of truth (Claude's
   email comprehension has a known failure history: missed items, confusion on booking
   threads; never build anything whose correctness silently depends on it).

## Process rules (how Daniel specs design)

- Daniel cannot spec a look in words, and rounds of yes/no questions do not work.
  He specs by reacting: "no, I don't like that" is the data. Keep rounds small (one
  screen at a time), use his real episodes and guests in every artifact, never
  re-show a rejected idea, and log every reaction here the day it happens.
- Brainstorm in words BEFORE building mocks (his explicit request, 2026-06-12).
  No new mockups until he says go.
- Vary the STORY a screen tells, not the paint. The 2026-06-12 round failed by
  offering four skins of the same number-grid layout; the next round must offer
  different ways of telling the day's story in one consistent calm skin.
