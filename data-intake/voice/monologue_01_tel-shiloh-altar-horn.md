S03 Ep006 Monologue

Buried beneath the soil of the hills of Ephraim in the land of Israel—long before Rome rose to power, before the philosophy of Greece took hold, and before David ever wore a crown—these stones were waiting to be discovered.

For far too long, the modern world has treated the biblical narrative as if it floats in the clouds like a myth, disconnected from the reality of human history. But the Bible is not a fairy tale. It is ANCHORED—anchored in real places, real blood, real worship, and real stone. It is the tangible historical record of the CREATOR God interacting with His creation.

And one of those stones has now spoken, though it had been silent for nearly three thousand years. At Tel Shiloh—the very place where Joshua chapter 18 tells us the "tabernacle of the congregation" was set up—archaeologists have uncovered something extraordinary:

The horn of an altar.

This is not a symbol. This is not a metaphor. This is a physical stone horn—cut, shaped, and used in the worship of the One who FORMED the heavens. This discovery was made in 2019 by Dr. Scott Stripling and the Associates for Biblical Research, right there on the northern slope of ancient Shiloh. But the location isn't the only thing that matters—it is the TIMING.

Crucially, this artifact was found sealed inside a destruction layer dating to the time of the Judges, the days of Eli and Samuel. This places the artifact BEFORE David, BEFORE Solomon, and BEFORE Jerusalem became the center of worship.

And what emerged from that layer would rewrite our understanding of early Israelite worship.

For years, academia INSISTED that Shiloh was nothing more than a backwoods village. They claimed that early Israel had no organized worship system. They argued that the priesthood, the sacrificial system, and the laws of Leviticus were all invented centuries later by elites to legitimize power in Jerusalem. But these stones don't lie.

What Dr. Stripling found were three stone altar horn fragments—not decorative, but structural—part of an actual working altar. As it is written in Leviticus chapter 4, "And the priest shall put some of the blood upon the horns of the altar."

This wasn't theory; this was one of the most impactful finds of the last century.

But the picture gets even more vibrant, the more the dig continues.

The structure they discovered–oriented east to west—just as the Tabernacle was described in Scripture—was large enough to SHATTER the myth that Shiloh was insignificant. Add to that a ceramic pomegranate, a priestly symbol sewn into the very robe of the High Priest. And then—perhaps most stunning of all—a massive bone deposit. Thousands of bones were carefully analyzed. And, as you'd expect, they were from kosher animals, young animals of sacrificial age, and predominantly from the RIGHT side of the body.

Think about what this means. In a normal meal, people consume both sides of an animal equally. The distribution would be random. But at Shiloh, the left sides were eaten—and the right sides were SET APART.

That pattern follows a specific code, found in Leviticus chapter 7: "And the right shoulder shall ye give unto the priest for an heave offering." This is not coincidence. This is biblical law in ACTION, centuries before critics say it ever existed. The text says the priests get the right side; the ground at Shiloh says the people gave the right side.

Furthermore, archaeology solves a textual puzzle regarding the structure itself. The Bible describes the sanctuary at Shiloh as both a tent and a temple—a Tabernacle with doors. Critics mocked this as a contradiction. But the stones suggest that the Tabernacle at Shiloh was no longer wandering; it likely rested on a stone foundation, a semi-permanent structure bridging the gap between Moses' tent and Solomon's Temple.

So what is the conclusion of the matter? It means the Bible didn't borrow history—history CONFIRMS the Bible. It means Israel's worship system wasn't invented late like the critics of Biblical history state—it was lived out, according to what the Word of God says.

It means Scripture is not theological fiction—it is HISTORICAL revelation given by the One who set the stars in motion.

The evidence is here. Once again, the earth has done exactly what God said it would do. It has cried out.

I'm David Rives and THIS is the Genesis Science Report!
