Show 1 · S03 Ep026 — The Maker's Hands and 3D Printers

Opening Monologue · 16 graphics (6 P1 / 10 P2)
Script — every highlight is one graphic's span (last sentence = RDC Last Line)

1Genesis 1:27. 2"So God CREATED man in His own image, in the image of God He created him." The very first thing we learn about ourselves is that we are MADE by a Maker. CREATED by a Creator.

3And hidden inside that one simple truth is something extraordinary: when we build, when we invent, when we shape raw stuff into something that did not exist before, we are ECHOING the very heartbeat of the One who spoke galaxies into being. I want to tell you a story tonight, about a machine that in just forty years has gone from a science fiction dream to changing the way we care for the sick, build our homes, and reach for the stars. I'm talking about 3D PRINTING.

4It started in 1983. A man named Chuck Hull figured out something simple but stunning: if you shine an ultraviolet light on a special liquid resin, that liquid HARDENS. Layer by layer, drop by drop, he could grow a solid object right out of a pool of goo. He called it stereolithography.

5At the same time, a graduate student named Carl Deckard was walking a different road. He was using a LASER to fuse together beds of fine powder. 6It may sound strange, but POWDER holds itself up. You can print shapes inside of shapes, gears already assembled, structures that are impossible to make any other way. Two men, two different paths, both REFLECTING the creativity born in heaven.

7Fast forward some years. Around 2013, the world went WILD for 3D printing. The magazines screamed that every home would have a printer. Factories would die. The future had arrived. And then it didn't. The printers were slow and finicky, churning out cheap plastic trinkets nobody really needed. The hype CRASHED and entire companies folded.

8But after that crash, the engineers got serious. They stopped chasing the toy market and turned to the work that MATTERED: aerospace, medicine, and manufacturing. Then in 2015, a company called Carbon broke the biggest wall of all, SPEED. Their technology grows objects continuously out of a pool of resin, up to a hundred times faster than the old way, and the parts came out STRONG in every direction. Adidas now prints the soles of running shoes this way, at scale.

9And while everyone was staring at the small stuff, engineers took the same idea, scaled it up to the size of a HOUSE, and started printing with CONCRETE. Entire neighborhoods in Texas, one hundred homes, rising from a robotic nozzle. Watch this: 10[Icon & Lennar clip plays]

11What an amazing technology. It is the same technology NASA is testing to build shelters on the MOON and on MARS. God gave us dirt and dust, and we are learning to build cities from it.

12Now I want to be honest with you; the headlines oversell this. When you print a metal part, you can't just walk away with it. You have to cut it off the build plate, bake out the stress, machine the surfaces smooth. The Star Trek replicator? 13[Star Trek montage plays]

14We are not there yet. 3D printing handles the impossible shapes, and traditional tools handle the finishing touches. Human INGENUITY at work! So where does this go next? Medicine: custom titanium implants made for one specific patient's spine, or living tissue being printed in laboratories, moving toward clinical trials right now. That isn't science fiction; that is happening.

15Then, what about deep space travel? Such as habitats grown from Martian soil. And finally, it will change aspects of our everyday lives, such as the replacement of a part for the appliance nobody makes anymore, or a custom brace for the runner with one leg shorter than the other.

16Here is the wonder of it all. Every breakthrough I just described came from a HUMAN MIND, a mind made in the IMAGE of the One who carved canyons and hung stars. When Chuck Hull stared at a pool of resin and saw a future, when Carl Deckard looked at powder and saw possibility, they were doing what we were CREATED to do: to create, and to build, to REFLECT their Maker. 3D printing isn't just a machine; it is the result of the intelligent Mind that made intelligent minds.

Show 2 · S03 Ep027 — Diversity in the Ancient Animal Kingdom

Opening Monologue · 12 graphics (1 P1 / 11 P2)
Script — every highlight is one graphic's span (last sentence = RDC Last Line)

1Today we are talking about three creatures of the ancient world. As we look around the modern world, it is astounding how many kinds of creatures there are. But how many times have you wondered what existed in ancient times, perhaps before the flood, or at least millennia before today?

2What we find is a sprawling, dazzling canvas of life, an EXPLOSION of diversity. We find a world that was bursting with fully-formed, endlessly inventive creatures, animals so strange and so specialized that they stop the experts in their tracks. Every one of them is a brushstroke from the hand of a Creator whose imagination simply has no end. Let me show you three creatures, all described by scientists just in the last year or so, that put that boundless creativity on full display.

3First, let's talk about the crocodile that walked the floodplains. We tend to think we've got crocodiles figured out, don't we? Low-slung, lurking at the waterline, eyes and nostrils poking up like a periscope. A solved problem.

4And then a team working down in Argentine Patagonia pulls a skull out of a rock, and we meet Kostensuchus atrox. This was a BEAST. Around eleven and a half feet long, a quarter of a ton in weight, with a broad, deep skull and big, blade-edged teeth. The researchers call it a hypercarnivore, an apex predator that lived right alongside the dinosaurs and very likely ate the medium-sized ones.

5In a modern crocodile, the eyes and nostrils ride high on the skull, perfect for lying submerged and waiting. Kostensuchus breaks that mold. Its nostrils faced FORWARD, its eyes sat out on the SIDES of a tall, deep head. The scientists who described it concluded this was no patient ambusher lurking in the shallows; its anatomy points to an animal that searched out its prey across the land, part of a whole "land of crocs" that once flourished across the southern continents.

6At this time, it seems there were small and giant crocodiles, meat-eaters and plant-eaters, water-stalkers and land-hunters, an entire spectrum of design built on a single, familiar frame. To me, that isn't the look of a blind accident. That's the look of a Designer working a theme through brilliant variations.

7Next, let's talk about what is being called the "Hell Pig" of the ancient plains, the Daeodon. Daeodon isn't a true pig at all. It thundered across North America in ancient times, a member of an extinct group called the entelodonts, categorized with the even-toed hoofed mammals. It was extraordinary. The largest stood around six feet at the shoulder, taller than a man, rivaling a bison, and carried a skull nearly three feet long. To hold that enormous head up, it grew tall spines over its shoulders that anchored a great muscular hump and a cable of neck muscle, the same engineering trick a bison uses today.

8And the behavior the fossils hint at is just as remarkable. Those jaws and thick teeth could CRUSH bone. Scientists have found "meat caches" out in Wyoming, leftovers stockpiled for later, with bite marks that match an entelodont's jaws. Many researchers think Daeodon made its living as a scavenger, and we have no living animal that fills quite the same shoes. Looking at Daeodon, you can't help but marvel at the sheer raw power packed into the creatures that have walked this earth.

9Last, let's look at a miniature masterpiece. This kind of brilliant design doesn't only show up on a giant scale. Sometimes it arrives in a package the size of a chicken. For a long time, a handful of little bones from northern Spain were assumed to be the remains of a baby, just a juvenile of some bigger dinosaur, not worth a second look.

10But when researchers ran the bone microstructure under the microscope, they got a surprise: this wasn't a youngster at all. It was a fully grown, mature ADULT, and it was barely the size of a chicken. They named it Foskeia pelendonum. For such a tiny creature, Foskeia had a startlingly sophisticated, highly specialized skull, unexpected anatomical innovations in an animal you could hold in your arms. The bone studies even suggest it grew fast, with a brisk metabolism more like a bird or a small mammal, living a quick, agile life in dense forests.

11The instinct is always to assume the small thing is the simple thing, the primitive thing. Foskeia refuses that completely. I see a Creator who poured just as much care and ingenuity into a chicken-sized dinosaur as into a towering one. The attention to detail doesn't shrink when the animal does.

12Put these three side by side and step back. The history of life on this earth is richer, stranger, and more mysterious and inventive than any of us can imagine. There are so many breathtaking ways to be alive, and we keep digging up more of them. So whatever comes out of the ground next, truly, the HEAVENS declare the glory of God.

Show 3 · S03 Ep028 — The Goddess in the Rubble of Laodicea

Opening Monologue · 16 graphics (8 P1 / 8 P2)
Script — every highlight is one graphic's span (last sentence = RDC Last Line)

1Earlier this year in southwestern Turkey, archaeologists found a statue of Athena face down. 2Not standing tall in some grand temple where you'd expect a goddess to be, but lying in the dirt, in a pile of rubble, behind the stage wall of a theater. On March 31, 2026, archaeologists working behind the old stage building came across something buried in the fill, lying face down. And when they rolled her over, there she was. Athena, just without her HEAD.

3She's a headless white marble statue, nearly six and a half feet tall, draped in a long flowing gown, with a cloak at her neck and the head of Medusa carved across her chest, ringed with serpents. Her head and hands are missing, but the work that's left takes your breath away. The folds of the fabric look soft enough to touch. Scholars say the style points to the age of Caesar Augustus, twenty-seven B.C. to fourteen A.D. In other words, the world of the New Testament.

4And here's a little detail I love. The back of the statue was left rough and unfinished because she was only ever meant to be seen from the front, tucked between columns. She stood in the West Theater, a soaring three-story stage where statues between the pillars told stories of Homer's tales, Odysseus and the Cyclops, the sea monster Scylla. A whole wall of gods and heroes, watching over the crowd.

5And of all the gods they could have dug up, it was Athena. Goddess of war, yes, but also the goddess of WEAVING. The goddess of the loom. Because Laodicea was a weaving town.

6Let me paint you the picture, because if you've spent any time in your Bible you have probably heard a bit about Laodicea. The city was founded in the third century B.C. by a king named Antiochus, who named it after his wife, Laodice, who is thought to have later poisoned her husband. Laodicea sat on a hill near two rivers, right on a major trade road, just a few miles from Colossae, yes, THAT Colossae, the one Paul wrote his letter to.

7And this was no sleepy village. At its peak it may have held more than a hundred thousand people, when most ancient cities had forty or fifty thousand. It had one of the biggest stadiums in the ancient world, and five marketplaces where most cities made do with one. Under Rome it became the leading city of two dozen surrounding towns; the Roman Senator and writer Cicero himself held court there.

8And it was RICH. Famous for three things: banking, with its own gold exchange; a glossy black wool woven into prized clothing; and a medical school that made an eye salve sold all across the Roman world. So rich, in fact, that when an earthquake flattened the city around A.D. 60, and Rome offered to help pay to rebuild it, the people of Laodicea said no thanks, we've got the money ourselves.

9And then there's the water. Laodicea had no spring of its own. Water came in through a long pipe from miles away, so full of minerals the engineers built little caps on the pipes just to scrape out the gunk. By the time it arrived, it was lukewarm and unpleasant to drink. Yes, LUKEWARM.

10Because to this very city, the risen Christ sent a letter through the Apostle John, written down in Revelation chapter three. And listen to how He speaks their language:

11To a banking town with a gold exchange, "buy from Me gold tried in the fire." 12To a city of fine wool, "white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed." 13To the makers of eye salve, "anoint thine eyes, that thou mayest see."

14And to a people choking down warm, mineral water, "So then because thou art lukewarm, and neither cold nor hot, I will spue thee out of my mouth." 15"Because thou sayest, I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing; and knowest not that thou art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked."

16Now think about that statue one MORE time. The goddess of the loom, in a city that grew wealthy at the loom. The old myths carved in beautiful marble, polished where the crowd could see, and rough and unfinished on the back where nobody would look. That's the very thing Christ was warning them about. Athena lay there, face down in the rubble, for two thousand years. The thing they trusted, toppled and buried in the dust. And the warning still stands, and even archaeology backs up God's word.

Show 4 · S03 Ep029 — Deep Sea Discoveries in Australia

Opening Monologue · 12 graphics (2 P1 / 10 P2)
Script — every highlight is one graphic's span (last sentence = RDC Last Line)

1"Scientists Discover Over 110 New Species in the Coral Sea." "Mysterious Creatures Pulled From Three Kilometers Beneath the Waves." "An Entire Hidden World Found Off the Coast of Australia." What these headlines describe is nothing short of ASTONISHING.

2The Coral Sea lies off the northeastern coast of Australia, and it is enormous beyond easy comprehension. The marine park alone covers nearly 390,000 square miles and plunges to depths of 20,000 feet. It shelters thirty-four sprawling reef systems and dozens of tiny islands scattered across the open water. It is the largest marine park in all of Australian waters, and yet here is the part that ought to HUMBLE us all: the vast majority of it had never been explored by anyone.

3We had mapped the surface of Mars and photographed the rings of distant Saturn, and all the while this immense living sea sat right here on our own planet, waiting in the dark, holding secrets.

4So a team of scientists set out to change that. For thirty-five days they sailed aboard a research vessel called the Investigator, lowering their instruments down through the water column, nearly two miles under water. That is a realm where sunlight has never reached and never will, a place of crushing pressure and perpetual cold and total darkness, and into that darkness they went looking for whatever might be living there.

5What they found exceeded all expectation. Marine scientists identified more than one hundred and ten species of fish and invertebrates entirely new to science, creatures that no human being had ever before named or described, and they tell us this number is likely to climb past two hundred as they continue to study the strange forms they carried back to the surface.

6Imagine that for a moment: more than two HUNDRED kinds of living things that were swimming in our oceans all this time, and we never knew a single one of them was there. God did. He made them. But we remained totally unaware until this discovery.

7The shark expert leading the expedition identified four new species himself, including two rays, a deepwater catshark, and a ghostly creature called a chimaera that haunts the deep.

8But the discoveries did not stop with the great fish, because the team also brought up brittle stars and crabs and delicate sea anemones, along with sponges and jellyfish and worms that came drifting from the waters beside the Great Barrier Reef and from remote underwater mountains rising lonely and unseen from the ocean floor. They also captured footage of a rare sand tiger shark, a creature of the deep, gliding through the blackness on a camera lowered into a world we have only just begun to glimpse.

9Now you might wonder how anyone confirms that a creature is truly new. These scientists studied the precise shape and structure of each animal, and then they read the letters of its DNA, the genetic signature written into every living thing that has ever existed.

10Consider now the scale of all that still remains unknown. Scientists have so far catalogued around two hundred and forty thousand marine species, which sounds like a tremendous number until you learn that they believe millions more are still hidden out there, millions of living things in the waters of this planet that have never been seen, never been named, never once been known to us.

11I find in all of this a kind of holy reminder. We worship a Creator with such creative magnificence. Because, let's be honest, we tend to walk around feeling certain that we understand our world, carrying the whole of human knowledge in our pockets, and then the ocean quietly opens up and shows us that we have barely even begun.

12A single voyage to a single sea turned up more than a hundred living marvels we never knew existed, and that fact alone should set our hearts racing at the thought of all that still waits down there in the dark, patient and silent, for the day we finally come looking. What a deep and wondrous world, revealing the most incredible God.

Show 5 · S03 Ep030 — Beneath the Antarctic Ice (10 Facts)

Opening Monologue · 15 graphics (11 P1 / 4 P2)
Script — every highlight is one graphic's span (last sentence = RDC Last Line)

1Imagine a place so vast, so utterly inhospitable, that it makes the Arctic look like a beach resort. Antarctica covers 5 million square miles, nearly the size of two Australias, and it's almost entirely covered in ice. 2Ninety percent of the world's ice is locked beneath that frozen surface. Seventy percent of our planet's fresh water. This is the last great wilderness on Earth, a continent so extreme that no permanent human settlements exist there. Only scientists, explorers, and the incredibly resilient creatures that call it home. But here's what most people don't realize: Antarctica isn't just a frozen wasteland. It's a time machine, and in 2025 there was an incredible discovery buried beneath the ice.

3FACT #1: A LOST WORLD BURIED BENEATH ICE. Over a mile beneath the Antarctic ice, scientists just discovered something that rewrites our understanding of this continent. Using satellite technology and ice-penetrating radar, researchers uncovered an ancient landscape, untouched for millennia. What they found was extraordinary: valleys and ridges carved by ancient rivers, a terrain roughly the size of Wales, shaped by flowing water. Perhaps before the flood, this part of the earth could have been like the Pacific Northwest or New Zealand. 4And there were palm trees, yes, PALM TREES in Antarctica. Scientists found pollen from them preserved in the ice, proof that this continent once teemed with life. It's like opening a time capsule of Earth itself, frozen at a moment when our planet was fundamentally different.

5FACT #2: BLOOD FALLS, WHERE THE GLACIER BLEEDS RED. There's a waterfall in Antarctica that looks like the continent is bleeding. Called Blood Falls, it emerges from the Taylor Glacier in streams of deep crimson red. Discovered in 1911, scientists were mystified for over 100 years. Finally they found the answer: the water is so saturated with iron oxide it turns rust-colored, and it supports life forms that have lived in complete darkness and isolation, an alien ecosystem that exists nowhere else on Earth.

6FACT #3: MOUNT EREBUS, THE ONLY VOLCANO IN ANTARCTICA, AND IT ERUPTS CONTINUOUSLY. Mount Erebus sits on Ross Island, the world's southernmost active volcano. It's been erupting continuously since 1972, lava fountains shooting hundreds of meters into subzero air, molten rock at 1,200 degrees meeting ice at minus 50. The volcano creates its own pocket of geothermal heat in Antarctica's frozen hell. Even in Earth's most extreme places, heat finds a way.

7FACT #4: WHERE THE SKY LITERALLY BURNS YOUR SKIN. Every Antarctic spring, UV radiation is so intense it can sunburn your skin in minutes. 8FACT #5: THE MCMURDO DRY VALLEYS, A PLACE MORE ALIEN THAN MARS. In the Taylor Valley, patches of ground likely haven't seen rain in millennia. The McMurdo Dry Valleys are the most extreme desert on Earth, colder and drier than Mars. Yet life thrives there: microbes in the soil, bacteria adapted to the cold with antifreeze proteins, frozen for millennia and able to simply wake up.

9FACT #6: SUBGLACIAL LAKES, 400 WORLDS HIDDEN BENEATH THE ICE. Over 400 lakes exist beneath the Antarctic ice sheet, completely isolated from sunlight. Lake Vostok, the largest, is roughly the size of Lake Ontario. Scientists have never drilled down to reach it; they don't want to contaminate it. But they know it's there. 10FACT #7: METEORITES RAIN DOWN FROM SPACE, AND ANTARCTICA COLLECTS THEM. Antarctica receives more meteorites per square mile than anywhere on Earth. Scientists have recovered over 20,000, fragments that traveled through space only to land in the one place where humans could actually find and study them.

11FACT #8: ANTIFREEZE FISH AND TRANSPARENT OCTOPUSES. Antarctic fish carry antifreeze proteins in their blood, actual molecules that prevent ice crystals from forming; their blood has a lower freezing point than freshwater. In these deeper waters, octopuses and squid have transparent heads. You can see their organs through their skin.

12FACT #9: THE ENDURANCE, A GHOST SHIP THAT HAUNTED HISTORY FOR 107 YEARS. In 1914, Ernest Shackleton's ship, the Endurance, became trapped in Antarctic pack ice. 13The crew drifted for nearly two years before abandoning it as the ice crushed the hull. For 107 years, no one knew where it was. Then, in 2022, researchers found it on the ocean floor, remarkably preserved, wood intact, artifacts undisturbed. The extreme cold and lack of wood-eating organisms froze the Endurance in time.

14FACT #10: THE POLAR VORTEX, ANTARCTICA AFFECTS YOUR WEATHER. The polar vortex is a jet stream phenomenon centered over Antarctica. When it wobbles, it sends ripples across the entire planet, Arctic air as far south as Florida. Blizzards. Ice storms. Dangerous cold snaps.

It's home to impossible life, impossible geology, impossible beauty. And now, with the recent discovery in 2025, we know how incredible this place was in God's original design.
