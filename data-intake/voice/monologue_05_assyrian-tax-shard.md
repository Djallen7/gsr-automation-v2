Show 5 Monologue

Today, we're heading straight into the heart of ancient Jerusalem with a discovery that's shaking up biblical archaeology.

Now, for the adults out there, taxes are ANYTHING but thrilling.

Get this – Just a few months ago, in October 2025, archaeologists unearthed a tiny pottery fragment—barely an inch across—near the Temple Mount's Western Wall. And it may be the most EXCITING tax discovery in history!

(Insert Clip)

This isn't just any old shard; it's inscribed with cuneiform writing, the wedge-shaped script of the ancient Near East, and it's the first royal Assyrian inscription EVER found in Jerusalem.

Dating back about 2,700 years to the 7th century BC, this piece of clay carries a message that reads like an overdue notice from the Assyrian Empire to the Kingdom of Judah.

Picture this: a high-ranking Assyrian official in the capital city of Nineveh writes a demand on a clay tablet. It mentions a delay in payment, sets a deadline and hints at consequences if the tribute isn't sent. It's not exactly the most inspiring letter, but nevertheless it is significant.

In fact, this shard is DIRECT evidence of the tense power dynamics between a superpower empire and a smaller vassal kingdom—Judah under the Assyrian thumb.

Now, here's where it gets truly BIBLICAL.

This artifact lines up perfectly with the historical record in the Word of God!

In 2 Kings 18, we read about King Hezekiah of Judah dealing with the Assyrian king Sennacherib. At first, Hezekiah pays up a massive tribute—300 talents of silver and 30 talents of gold—to keep the peace.

But later, he rebels, sparking a full invasion.

The Bible puts it this way: "And the king of Assyria appointed unto Hezekiah king of Judah three hundred talents of silver and thirty talents of gold. And Hezekiah gave him ALL the silver that was found in the house of the Lord, and in the treasures of the king's house." (2 Kings 18:14-15)

This discovery could be a snapshot of that very pressure— a "pay up or else" memo that echoes the biblical tension.

Over the years, archaeology has confirmed biblical kings, cities, and events time and again—from the House of David inscription to the ruins of Jericho.

We have spoken of many of these examples in other episodes of the Genesis Science Report.

This Assyrian tax notice adds another LAYER.

Taxes may seem boring, but Jesus said that not one jot or tittle would pass away from God's word. And this is a perfect example of that being true.

This proves that the names, places, and even power struggles can be proven accurate.

They're documented, right down to the bureaucratic HEADACHES of ancient taxation.

Experts date it to the late 8th or mid-7th century BC, smack in the era of Hezekiah's reign.

But let's zoom out.

Why does a tiny tax shard matter in the grand scheme? Because if the Bible nails these historical facts with such precision, it builds trust in its bigger picture—the account of creation itself, the patriarchs, the Judges, the Kings, the ancient tales of wars, victories, judgements…Yes, it's all true, every bit.

Each dig reveals how God's Word stands the test of time, from the details of royal rebellions to the origins of the universe.

And speaking of God's involvement, this find spotlights Him in multiple roles.

He's the SOVEREIGN over nations, raising up Assyria as an instrument of judgment, as Isaiah 10:5 calls it: "O Assyrian, the rod of mine anger."

He's the preserver of TRUTH, burying evidence in the earth for millennia only to reveal it now, when skeptics question His Word.

He's the HISTORIAN who records events through prophets like those in Kings, ensuring accuracy that science later affirms.

And ultimately, He's the Creator who set the stage for all this—forming the clay from which this sherd was made, just as He formed Adam from the dust.

I'm David Rives, and THIS is the Genesis Science Report
