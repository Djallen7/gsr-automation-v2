S03 Ep009 Monologue

We have reported on ancient civilizations in the past, connecting the dots between the pre-Flood world, and early development of civilizations after that global cataclysm.

A big part of our argument in the past is that if Darwinian evolution was true, then how would such sophistication be happening in our ancient past?

But time and again, what is coming to the surface is that high levels of sophistication are being found where there should only be PRIMITIVE signs of life.

Now for the latest discoveries:Off the rugged coast of Brittany, France—near a small island called Île de Sein—divers made a discovery that should stop every textbook editor in their tracks. Beneath nine meters of Atlantic water lies a massive granite wall, stretching nearly 400 feet long, built from over 3,300 TONS of stone.

This is the headline from the BBC: Huge undersea wall dating from 5000 BC found in France.

This is not random rubble. This is ENGINEERING.

The structure—now called TAF1—was explored between 2022 and 2024, and what archaeologists found rewrites the story of early human capability.

These ancient builders began with a bold idea: they set more than sixty upright granite monoliths directly into bedrock—some aligned in parallel rows—forming a rigid skeletal framework. Only after that did they infill the structure with enormous stone blocks and vertical slabs.

This wasn't guesswork. This was DESIGN.

And here's what makes it even more remarkable: this coastal wall predates the famous megaliths of Carnac, inland France's iconic stone alignments, by centuries. In other words, the blueprint for monumental stone architecture may not have begun inland—but at the sea's edge.

Now ask yourself: Who were these people supposed to be? We've been told for generations that Mesolithic humans were primitive—nomadic bands armed with little more than flint knives, scraping out a fragile existence.

But you don't quarry, transport, and precisely place thousands of tons of granite without planning, leadership, communication, and shared purpose.

This was not caveman CHAOS.

This was calculated COORDINATION.

We see this same pattern of ARCHITECTURE appearing out of nowhere across the globe. Take for instance Gobekli Tepe in Turkey. Long before the dates assigned to the first cities, humans were carving massive T-shaped pillars with intricate reliefs of animals and celestial symbols.

Like this wall in France, it defies the secular humanist timeline. It proves that the human spirit did not slowly "evolve" the ability to organize; it arrived on the scene with that ABILITY already intact.

Even after the world was broken by the global judgment of the Flood, that divine spark of INTELLIGENCE remained. The survivors did not become "cavemen" in the sense of losing their humanity; they became pioneers in a scarred world.

The wall spans a natural coastal valley between high and low tide marks. Researchers believe it may have functioned as an advanced fish-trapping system, with monoliths supporting nets as tides receded—or as an early form of sea defense, protecting vital land from encroaching waters. Either way, this was not survival by chance.

This was STRATEGY. In a world reshaped by cataclysmic forces, these communities didn't lose their creativity or capacity for genius.

They reshaped their landscape, turning post-catastrophe challenges into INNOVATION. Genesis 1:28 tells us that humanity was given a mandate: "And God blessed them, and God said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and REPLENISH the earth, and SUBDUE it: and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth."

Here, submerged beneath the sea, we see that mandate lived out in stone. They were subduing a rising tide with three thousand TONS of granite.

We may not know the full story – but we can appreciate God's creativity expressed through them.

This discovery also sheds light on social structure.

These were not wandering tribes passing through. These were SEDENTARY coastal communities, sustained by the sea's abundance. Long before the plow reached this region, people were building permanent INFRASTRUCTURE—complex and durable.

And intriguingly, archaeology may be brushing up against legend. Local Breton lore speaks of Ys, a prosperous coastal city protected by a great sea wall—lost to the ocean.

Could memories of rising waters and abandoned engineered landscapes have passed down through generations, transforming history into myth? Perhaps more will be known, but for now it will remain a mystery.

Regardless of the truth of this myth – When we place this discovery beside conventional assumptions, the contrast is STUNNING. We see organized societies instead of small bands, granite engineering instead of crude shelters, and environmental mastery instead of helplessness.

The submerged wall of Île de Sein is more than an archaeological curiosity. It is a reminder that humanity did not crawl upward from ignorance—we carried INGENUITY with us from the beginning.

We are created with purpose, endowed with intelligence, and the capacity to build.

And sometimes, the strongest evidence isn't written on a page. It's written in stone—waiting beneath the waves.

I'm David Rives and THIS is the Genesis Science Report!
