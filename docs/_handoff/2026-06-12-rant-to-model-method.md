# The enablement method — how Claude learns Daniel's job (REVISED 2026-06-13)

Daniel's condition for investing months: a method he and Claude both agree will get
Claude to a competent, reliable, trustworthy production assistant, WITHOUT his
communication style (long ADHD rambles, apparent self-contradictions) poisoning the
records, and without forcing him into a heavy daily commitment.

> **2026-06-13 pivot (governs).** The original version of this doc made a daily raw
> "brain-dump journal" the ENGINE: Daniel would dictate every day and re-read/correct
> Claude's interpretations. He rejected that, correctly: the review burden is too high to
> sustain for two months, and it front-loads the work onto him. It was also the same
> churn pattern as the UI rounds. The journal is now demoted to an OPTIONAL inbox. The
> engine is below.

## The engine: build on paper, learn from use (not from narration)

The mistake we kept making was trying to MODEL Daniel's brain before building anything.
Both the month-map idea and the daily journal said "first you explain your whole world,
then I build." That is backwards, high-burden, and the reason we churned. The expert
move is the reverse:

1. **Build the surfaces that stand on facts that already exist on paper.** These need
   nothing from Daniel's head, and he can check Claude's work against the same documents.
   Order:
   - **Distribution tracker (FIRST).** Stands on the s11 platform registry, episode air
     dates, and the Monday 4PM ET webstream rhythm. Myriam's lane, so zero on-air risk to
     David. The cleanest proof that the dashboard can show true status with nothing
     narrated.
   - **Booking state-board (SECOND).** Each guest invite is a card with a state (idea →
     asked → waiting → date floated → confirmed → taped). Emails are ATTACHED EVIDENCE on
     the card, never the source of truth. This directly attacks the trust wound: Claude's
     known failure is losing the thread in email, and a visible board converts a silent
     miss into a card visibly sitting too long.
   - **Graphics + post trackers.** Stand on the Graphics Tracker sheet, Rundown Creator,
     and export artifacts.

2. **Model Daniel's own workflow by OBSERVING his actions, not by asking him to narrate.**
   Every time he uses the system (approves a lower third, advances an episode, books a
   guest), that is a free data point about his real workflow and ordering, captured with
   zero extra effort from him. Patterns (what he does before what, how long things sit)
   accrete over weeks. Claude surfaces them back as occasional 5-second confirmations
   ("Looks like you always lock lower thirds before booking the next guest — right?"),
   never as daily homework.

3. **The month map accretes; it is not authored upfront.** Crew tracks fill confidently
   from documents now. Daniel's own tracks fill from observed use over time. The dashboard
   home page (Lane 1) is composed LAST, from the tracks he actually uses.

## What this asks of Daniel (the whole burden)

- **Use the tools when Claude ships them, and correct them when they're wrong.** Seconds,
  in the flow of work he already does.
- That's it. No daily dictation requirement, no re-reading his own entries, no
  adjudicating his own contradictions, no explaining his life.

## The journal, demoted (optional inbox)

`docs/_handoff/PRODUCER-JOURNAL.md` stays as a zero-pressure inbox: if Daniel has a
thought, he dumps it (ideally into a pinned phone note that autosaves, so a call can't
wipe it like on 2026-06-12), and Claude files it raw + dated and extracts what it can. He
is NEVER obligated to review it, and if he never uses it the system still progresses via
the engine above. When he does dump, the old discipline still applies so nothing rots:
raw first, every extracted item tagged ALWAYS / USUALLY / NOW with his words as the
receipt, contradictions flagged and held as data (recency wins for plain facts; routine
conflicts mean "it depends" and stay open until a pattern shows or real work is blocked),
and a max-5-line echo receipt back.

## Honest limits

The system learns what it can SEE (actions taken inside it). It cannot see decisions made
on the phone, in Daniel's head, or in other tools. We widen what it can see over time
(more tracks, more integrations); for the rest, rare quick confirmations, not narration.

## The trust test (measurable)

When Daniel's corrections to what the system shows him approach zero, the priority lists
he could not trust before become trustworthy — because every line carries a receipt he
can check. Trust comes from checkable, never from "Claude remembers."
