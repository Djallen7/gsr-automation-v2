# Lower-Thirds Style Guide — The Genesis Science Report

**Status:** Producer reference. Reflects the locked rules as of 2026-05-27 and the corpus they were derived from.
**Source:** Synthesis of two conversation archives — Claude.ai export (346 conversations, 77 L3-touching, Apr–May 2026) and ChatGPT export (592 conversations, 144 L3-touching, Feb 2025 – Apr 2026). A third archive (Multisource Forensic Data) was forensic metadata only and contained no L3 content; it surfaced filename evidence that ministry-report L3s are tracked as a separate doc family and that the L3 template has 10+ stale duplicates worth deduping.

This guide is what to write **today**. The "Evolution log" at the bottom explains why earlier shows look different.

---

## 1. The one-page rules card

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Capitalization | ALL CAPS, every L3, no exceptions |
| Target length | 60–65 characters (aim 65; cluster 59–65) |
| Hard ceiling | 70 characters |
| Forbidden punctuation | Em-dashes (`—`), periods (`.`), commas (`,`) |
| Permitted punctuation | Colons (`:`), pipes (`\|`), question marks (`?`), apostrophes (`'`), ampersand (`&`) in chyrons only, plus sign (`+`) as topic separator |
| Topic-list separator (intros) | ` + ` — never `&` or `,` |
| Chyron separator | ` \| ` — three slots, space-padded |
| Rows per monologue segment | 15 (split: 10 specific + 5 evergreen safety nets) |
| Rows per ministry report | 3 (×3 variants = 3×3 grid) |
| Rows per segway / transition | 1 (×3 variants = 3×1 grid) |
| Variant columns | Primary / Var 1 / Var 2 (three columns per row) |
| Spoken-audio test | If a viewer hears the closer line and never sees the screen, the L3 should still make sense |
| Stand-alone test | If a viewer tunes in cold mid-segment and reads the L3, they know what's being discussed |

## 2. Segment-by-segment format

### 2.1 Episode intro (opening monologue)

**L3 #1 — the anchor.** `ON TONIGHT'S SHOW: [HOOK A] + [HOOK B]`
Two interview hooks joined by ` + `. Carries the show's promise. Length ceiling is 70 here even though the rest of the segment caps at 65 — the anchor is allowed to run long.

Canonical examples:
- `ON TONIGHT'S SHOW: EARTH'S TWIN AND THE CODE INSIDE EVERY CELL` (62)
- `ON TONIGHT'S SHOW: MARS "SKULL HILL" + CAMBRIAN EXPLOSION` (59)
- `ON TONIGHT'S SHOW: CRACKS IN EVOLUTIONARY THEORY + EVIDENCE WRITTEN IN THE HEAVENS` (82, only acceptable on the anchor)

Variation is allowed — drop the literal phrase if it fits the night better. `IN TONIGHT'S LINEUP`, `TONIGHT ON GSR`, `COMING UP` are all live. Just keep the two-hook structure.

**L3 #2 — the evergreen headline.** A monologue-topic headline that works without context. Triggerable any time during the open. This is the line that lets a late tune-in viewer catch up in two seconds.

**L3s #3–10 — specific progression.** Each L3 advances the monologue's argument one beat. They must not be reorderable. If you can shuffle them, they're describing the topic instead of progressing through it. Each L3 should be a news headline that pairs with the on-screen talk happening underneath it.

**L3s #11–15 — generic safety nets.** Evergreen lines that ride during graphics rolls, b-roll, anywhere the operator needs a fallback. They sound like cable-news teases:

- `WHAT THE SCIENCE ACTUALLY SHOWS`
- `A CLOSER LOOK AT THE EVIDENCE`
- `WHAT MAINSTREAM COVERAGE IS NOT TELLING YOU`
- `THE QUESTION THE HEADLINES ARE NOT ASKING`
- `WHERE THE DATA POINTS WHEN YOU FOLLOW IT`

### 2.2 Guest interview

Each interview segment gets:

1. **Topic L3** — a single grabby headline that previews the interview. 60–65 chars. Same rules as a monologue progression L3.
2. **Guest chyron** — three slots separated by ` | `. See §3.
3. **15 discussion L3s** — 10 specific + 5 safety nets, same structure as the monologue.

Topic L3s with Var 1 / Var 2:

| Primary | Var 1 | Var 2 |
|---|---|---|
| `A MICROBE THAT REWRITES THE RULES OF THE GENETIC CODE` | `WHEN A STOP SIGNAL MEANS KEEP GOING AND KEEP BUILDING` | `BERKELEY FINDS A CRACK IN BIOLOGY'S MOST FUNDAMENTAL RULE` |
| `NASA NUCLEAR SPACECRAFT IS HEADED FOR MARS` | `70 YEARS IN THE MAKING AND IT IS FINALLY HAPPENING` | `FIRST NUCLEAR PROPELLED SHIP TO LEAVE EARTH'S ORBIT` |
| `THE NIGHT SKY AT RISK` | `A STARTUP WANTS TO SELL SUNLIGHT AFTER DARK` | `THE FCC IS DECIDING THE FUTURE OF THE NIGHT SKY` |

### 2.3 Ministry report

3 rows × 3 variants. Same tone as monologue — newsy and plainspoken, not pastoral. Lead with people and outcomes, not the ministry's own actions.

Canonical examples:
- `OVER 40,000 YOUTUBE SUBSCRIBERS — A NEW MILESTONE` *(em-dash; do not reuse this exact form)*
- `WONDERS CENTER WELCOMES NEW RESIDENT DINGUS THE TORTOISE` (52)
- `PUBLIC SCHOOL STUDENTS VISIT THE WONDERS CENTER` (47)
- `PARTNER WITH US TO BRING BIBLICAL SCIENCE TO MORE PEOPLE` (56)

Ministry-report L3s live in their own document/template family (`GSR_Ministry_Report_Lower_Thirds`), not the monologue L3 sheet.

### 2.4 Q&A / Kids' Corner / Featured Resource / Viewer Voices / Heavens Declare / Genesis Science Minute

Short flag-style L3s — 1 row × 3 variants per segment. Same length and punctuation rules. Tone matches the segment: Kids' Corner is allowed to use the exclamation point liberally (`SURVIVAL IN -58°F! GOD EQUIPPED THE ARCTIC FOX PERFECTLY`). Heavens Declare is the one place the biblical close-out tone is the default rather than the exception (`THE HEAVENS STILL DECLARE THE GLORY OF GOD`).

### 2.5 Segway / transition between segments

1 row × 3 variants. Short. Doesn't preview, doesn't summarize — pivots. Avoid "we're continuing" phrasing.

## 3. Guest chyrons

**Format:** `NAME | AFFILIATION | RELEVANCE`

Three slots, separated by ` | ` (pipe with spaces on both sides). 50–65 characters total. Slash (`/`) is wrong — always pipe.

The third slot is **positioning**, not just an org. Ask: *why should the viewer care about the next 13 minutes?*

Worked example — the same guest, three takes, only the third works:

| Attempt | Result | Chars |
|---|---|---|
| First | `DR. MICHAEL HOUTS \| NUCLEAR RESEARCH MGR NASA MARSHALL \| PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR SPACE NUCLEAR PROPULSION` | 101 ❌ |
| Second | `DR. MICHAEL HOUTS \| NUCLEAR RESEARCH MANAGER, NASA MARSHALL \| AUTHOR, EVOLUTION IS RELIGION NOT SCIENCE` | 100 ❌ |
| **Final** | `DR. MICHAEL HOUTS \| NASA \| SPACE NUCLEAR PROPULSION` | **51 ✓** |

The fix: aggressively trim credentials, drop the long org name, and make the third slot tell the viewer what this interview is *about*.

Other locked chyrons:
- `DR. ANDREW FABICH | MICROBIOLOGIST | CITED IN NATURE AND SCIENCE` (64)
- `BILLY HALLOWELL | CBN JOURNALIST | "ANGELS & DEMONS" FILMMAKER` (62)

When the positioning move is to name a book or film, do it — that's stronger than an org affiliation:

- `DR. JEFF TOMKINS | GENETICIST | AUTHOR, THE DESIGN AND COMPLEXITY OF THE CELL` (preferred)
- `DR. JEFF TOMKINS | GENETICIST | ICR` (acceptable but flat)

## 4. Tone

Four registers — pick deliberately. Most L3s sit in 1 and 2.

1. **Newsy / cable.** The default. Declarative, present tense, active voice.
   - `32 MONTHS TO LAUNCH AND THE CLOCK IS ALREADY RUNNING`
   - `PNAS STUDY: HIGH-YIELD FARMING PREVENTED 590 GIGATONS OF CO2`
   - `BERKELEY FINDS A CRACK IN BIOLOGY'S MOST FUNDAMENTAL RULE`

2. **Standalone-grabby topic.** For Topic L3s and L3 #2. Carries a full thought even cold.
   - `WHEN A CBN REPORTER INVESTIGATED UFOs HE FOUND DEMONS`
   - `THE WARNING TO THE CHURCH HIDDEN INSIDE UFO DISCLOSURE`

3. **Evergreen safety net.** For rows 11–15. No specifics — generic enough to ride during any beat.
   - `WHAT THE SCIENCE ACTUALLY SHOWS`
   - `A CLOSER LOOK AT THE EVIDENCE`

4. **Biblical close-out.** For Heavens Declare and end-of-segment cues. Sparing elsewhere — don't apply to non-Christian guests' segments.
   - `THE HEAVENS STILL DECLARE THE GLORY OF GOD`
   - `INFORMATION ALWAYS TRACES BACK TO A MIND`

### Patterns to lean on

- **Guest-name prefix** for discussion L3s: `WERNER: HUMAN EVOLUTION ICONS BUILT ON SHAKY GROUND`, `DEYOUNG: WHY REDSHIFT MAY NOT MEAN EXPANSION`
- **Source prefix:** `PNAS STUDY:`, `NOBEL ECONOMIST:`, `BERKELEY:`
- **Two-beat construction** without a period: `ONE CODON TWO PROTEINS NO KNOWN MECHANISM`
- **Rhetorical question, broadcast-style:** `COULD ALIENS BE TERRAFORMING PLANETS?`

### Patterns to avoid

- **"Too clever" wordplay.** *"Less clever and more like grabby news headlines"* — Daniel, 2025-02-05.
- **Topic-label L3s.** `THE GENETIC CODE` is a label, not a headline. Make it news.
- **Repeating opener structures within a segment.** If three lines in a row begin `WHEN…` or `WHAT…`, vary them.
- **Narration over progression.** *"If you could rearrange them in any order and it wouldn't matter, they are not progressing."* — Daniel, 2026-04-16 self-critique.
- **Comma-as-pause.** Use line break in the script, not a comma in the L3.

## 5. Failure modes — corrections from the archive

Each row is a real before/after. The "why" column is the producer's stated reason where available.

| Before (draft) | After / rule | Why |
|---|---|---|
| `MAMMALS IN 9 EASY STEPS? NOT SO FAST...` | rewrite as grabby news headline | Too clever, not newsy |
| 7 L3s opening with `WHAT'S REALLY…` / `ARE THESE…` / `WHEN SCIENCE…` | vary openers and lengths | "All essentially saying things that are too similar" |
| Summary lines like `MOST SOILS DO NOT BEGIN AS BEDROCK` | rewrite to news-headline that gives mid-segment context | "Need to act as news headlines that go with the convo" |
| `DR DON DEYOUNG \| EMERITUS PHYSICS & ASTRONOMY PROFESSOR \| CREATION SCIENCE AUTHOR` (80+) | trim to 60 chars, do real research on the guest first | Chyrons must fit; flat credentials are weak |
| `UFO DISCLOSURE IS COMING. THE CHURCH ISN'T READY.` | strip periods, use line break instead | "There should not be periods in the Lower thirds" |
| `THE MORE WE STUDY THE CELL, THE MORE DESIGN WE FIND` | strip comma | Comma-purge May 2026 |
| `WHEN A STOP SIGN MEANS KEEP GOING — AND KEEP BUILDING` | strip em-dash | Em-dash purge Sept 2025 — but Claude still slipped this in April 2026, so audit every pass |
| `ARTICLE CLAIMS SOIL FORMS SLOWLY` (32 chars) | expand to ~60 | "Some of those L3s are too short. Keep them closer to 60" |
| `IN TONIGHT'S LINEUP: WHO PROGRAMS THE FUTURE? + WHAT IF THE TREE OF LIFE IS WRONG?` (85) | trim to ≤70 | Ceiling on intro anchor |
| OUTRO L3 section after Q&A | delete | "We don't need outro L3s for these next interviews" |
| Three-item list relying on commas (`AWE: ADVENTURE, WORSHIP, EDUCATION`) | use AND chains or restructure | Cost of the no-comma rule |
| `DR. JEFF TOMKINS \| GENETICIST \| ICR` | swap third slot for book/film/positioning | Flat affiliation < relevance |

## 6. Per-segment variant grid (operational)

For each episode the L3 deliverable is:

```
Opening monologue:    15 rows × 3 variants (Primary / Var 1 / Var 2)
Interview 1 topic:     1 row  × 3 variants
Interview 1 chyron:    1 row  (no variants)
Interview 1 discussion:15 rows × 3 variants (10 specific + 5 safety net)
Interview 2 topic:     1 row  × 3 variants
Interview 2 chyron:    1 row
Interview 2 discussion:15 rows × 3 variants
Ministry report:       3 rows × 3 variants
Kids' Corner:          1 row  × 3 variants
Q&A:                   1 row  × 3 variants
Featured Resource:     1 row  × 3 variants
Heavens Declare:       1 row  × 3 variants
Segway × N:            1 row  × 3 variants per transition
```

The dashboard import schema (`/api/import`) expects flat JSON arrays, not nested wrappers. The Supabase `lower_thirds` table is the source of truth — `/upload` (PNG) is being phased out as of 2026-05-27.

JSON shape (from the May 26 scraping schema):

```json
{
  "season": 3,
  "episode_number": 47,
  "title": "EPISODE TITLE",
  "guest_name": "DR. ANDREW FABICH",
  "segment": "interview_1",
  "beat_number": 4,
  "initial_text": "THE ORGANISM READS THE STOP SIGNAL TWO WAYS AND THRIVES"
}
```

`segment` enum (locked): `opening_monologue`, `interview_1`, `interview_2`, `kids_corner`, `genesis_science_qa`, `ministry_report`, `viewer_voices`, `featured_resource`, `heavens_declare`, `genesis_science_minute`, `other`.

## 8. Operator's checklist before submitting an episode's L3s

1. Every line is ALL CAPS.
2. Every line is 60–65 characters (anchor L3 can run to 70).
3. No em-dashes, no periods, no commas in any L3.
4. Topic L3 separator is ` + `, chyron separator is ` | `.
5. Anchor L3 names both interview hooks.
6. L3 #2 works as a standalone evergreen headline.
7. L3s #3–10 are not reorderable — each advances the argument.
8. L3s #11–15 are evergreen safety nets.
9. Guest chyron is three pipe-separated slots, ≤65 chars, third slot is positioning.
10. Ministry report L3s lead with people/outcomes, not the ministry's actions.
11. No Christian framing on non-Christian guests' interview L3s (David applies that in his sign-off).
12. The closer L3 does not place `creationsuperstore.com` mid-line — that's a script CTA, not an L3.
13. Every L3 passes the stand-alone test (cold viewer can follow) and the spoken-audio test (line makes sense without graphics).
14. JSON import is dry-run first; live `/api/import` requires explicit producer confirmation per `CLAUDE.md`.
